About the Xeno Championship

Welcome to the NMS Xeno Championship

The NMS Xeno Championship is an annual fan-run tournament celebrating the competitive spirit of No Man's Sky players. Since its inception, this championship has brought together explorers from across the galaxy to battle in the thrilling Xeno Arena.

Compete in intense 1v1 best-of-3 fixtures, showcase your piloting skills, and climb the rankings to join the prestigious Hall of Fame!

What is Xeno Arena?

Xeno Arena is No Man's Sky's competitive multiplayer mode where pilots engage in fast-paced space battles. Using starships equipped with various weapons and upgrades, players must outmaneuver and outgun their opponents in a race to claim victory.

The championship focuses on fair, skill-based competition while maintaining the exploratory and creative freedom that makes No Man's Sky special.

Community Driven

This tournament is entirely fan-run and community-driven. From the organizers to the participants, everyone contributes to making the NMS Xeno Championship a memorable event for the No Man's Sky community.

Join us in celebrating the game's competitive potential and connecting with fellow explorers!

Championship Rules

Registration

In registering to participate in this tournament, each player submits the following information:

  • Full Name (Kept private IF Display Name is also provided)
  • Display Name (Optional)
  • Email Address (Kept private)
  • Platform Name (May be shown on stream)
  • NMS Friend Code (Kept private)
  • Weekday Time Slot Availability (Kept private)

Information gathered on registration is primarily for logistical purposes, unless stated otherwise. Fixture time slots are then also assigned based on the submitted preference (where applicable) and on a 'first come, first serve' basis. If a specified time slot is no longer available, the registrant will be given a selection of other available time slots to choose from.

Match Format

Once registered, every Xeno Arena fixture (a 1v1 best of 3) will be hosted by a designated tournament umpire. This means that each fixture's participants join the umpire's party in the Space Anomaly on the pre-arranged date and time, allowing the umpire to monitor the wins / losses in real time.

Each fixture is also required to be streamed by at least one party (though both are allowed to do so) - this is to allow for the hosting and monitoring of the match by spectators.

At the end of a fixture the umpire will then record the result of the fixture, along with a verifying clip from the live stream - in order to avoid any doubt in the authenticity of a fixture's result.

Scheduling

Fixtures will always be scheduled on weekdays, in line with UK time (UTC+01:00).

Tournament Structure

The Qualification Stage consists of five (5) distinct rounds, with sixteen (16) fixtures per round, for a total of eighty (80) fixtures for this stage. Fixtures for the Qualification Stage will be scheduled at four (4) fixtures per day. This scheduling is to ensure that each of the five (5) rounds are completed within 4 days of starting - with one (1) day between rounds for administration - lasting for a total of twenty-four (24) days for this stage.

During the Qualification Stage players will accumulate points based on the outcomes of their fixtures, these points will determine if they qualify for placement in the Top 8.

Points for the Qualification Stage are won / deducted corresponding with the following results:

  • 1 Point - Winning a fixture.
  • 0 Points - Losing a fixture.
  • -1 Point - Fixture no notice / no show (after 10 minutes).

With so many fixtures in the Qualification Stage point ties are almost unavoidable, and so any ties in points at the end of the fifth round will be resolved by determining which participant has a higher Fixture Win Percentage. If this still results in a tie then the participant with the most surviving companions in their last game will take the higher ranking, in the unlikely event there is still a tie after all of those checks then a sudden death deciding fixture will be arranged.

At the end of the qualification stage, the accumulated points and aforementioned tiebreakers will determine the placements and matchmaking for the Top 8 playoffs - this stage will switch to a single elimination style, with a final champion being left standing at the end.
