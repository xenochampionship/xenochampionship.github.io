About the Xeno Championship

Welcome to the NMS Xeno Championship

The NMS Xeno Championship is an annual fan-run tournament celebrating the competitive spirit of No Man's Sky players. Since its inception, this championship has brought together explorers from across the galaxy to battle in the thrilling Xeno Arena.

Compete in intense 1v1 best-of-3 fixtures, showcase your piloting skills, and climb the rankings to join the prestigious Hall of Fame!

Charitable Event

This year the Xeno Championship is proudly raising money in aid of Cancer Research UK in a concerted effort to combat cancer and support those most deeply affected by it.

Due to the tournament being free to enter, and each fixture being live streamed, it is highly encouraged that spectators and ontestants donate what they can. This is not a requirement, however the entire goal is to make a positive impact and a real difference in the fight against cancer.

Charitable donations are made and received via a dedicated Cancer Research UK Giving Page, with the specific donation link for the associated tournament becoming available once contestant registration window opens. At the conclusion of the tournament all proceeds will be donated to Cancer Research UK.

What is Xeno Arena?

Xeno Arena is No Man's Sky's competitive multiplayer mode where pilots engage in fast-paced space battles. Using starships equipped with various weapons and upgrades, players must outmaneuver and outgun their opponents in a race to claim victory.

The championship focuses on fair, skill-based competition while maintaining the exploratory and creative freedom that makes No Man's Sky special.

Community Driven

This tournament is entirely fan-run and community-driven. From the organizers to the participants, everyone contributes to making the NMS Xeno Championship a memorable event for the No Man's Sky community.

Join us in celebrating the game's competitive potential and connecting with fellow explorers!

Championship Rules

Tournament Format

The Xeno Championship tournament itself is comprised of up to 32 contestants, battling it out over 2 stages, and all competing for the top spot of Xeno Champion.

The first stage is a swiss style Qualification stage that all contestants are entered into. This stage takes place over 5 rounds, with the top 8 ranked contestants advancing through to the Finals.

Once the top 8 contestants have been determined through the Qualification stage they then enter the Finals, a single elimination knockout stage that concludes with deciding the top 3 (1st, 2nd and 3rd places).

Fixture Format

Each individual fixture is formatted in a 1v1 (best of 3) style, with the first contestant to achieve 2 Xeno Arena victories being awarded the fixture win.

Every fixture is to be streamed live on Twitch by at least 1 of the fixture's participants, and monitored by at least 1 predetermined tournament Umpire. The Umpire's job is to act as a scheduling intermediary and report the results of said fixtures once they have concluded.

Umpire's are furnished with the NMS Friend Codes of both contestants in order to invite them both into the same party and meet in the sam Space Anomaly session, allowing for guaranteed fixtures and the ability for the Umpire to physically monitor the outcomes of individual Xeno Arena battles.

Event Scheduling

The Xeno Championship takes place throughout the month of August and is divided into two scheduling formats: a flexible, player coordinated Qualification stage, followed by a fixed scheduling for the Finals.

Qualification Stage Scheduling

As the Qualification stage is played over 5 rounds, each round takes place within a 4-day match window.

Prior to the start of each round, fixtures will be generated and shared with contestants. Players are then required to coordinate with their assigned opponent to agree on a specific date and time for their match within the allotted window.

  • For Round 1, all foxtures must be scheduled before the tournament begins. Failure to do so may result in forfeiture of a contestant's place in the tournament.
  • For subsequent rounds, a 2-day scheduling period is provided between rounds to allow players to arrange their next fixture.

While an assigned Umpire will assist in facilitating scheduling via Discord, the responsibility ultimately lies with both contestants to communicate promptly and confirm a suitable date and time within the given timeframe.

Failure to schedule or complete a fixture within the allotted window may result in a forfeit, and recorded as a Fixture No Show.

Finals Scheduling

The Finals stage follows a fixed schedule and takes place across two days:

  • 30th August - Quarter-finals (Top 8)
  • 31st August - Semi-finals, Third Place Match, and Grand Final

Due to broadcast and production requirements, all Finals fixtures must be played at their assigned times and are expected to be live streamed on Twitch.

Each day will follow a structured schedule:

  • 11:00 - 12:00 (Fixture 1)
  • 13:00 - 14:00 (Fixture 2)
  • 15:00 - 16:00 (Fixture 3)
  • 17:00 - 18:00 (Fixture 4)

Player Availability

By registering for the Xeno Championship, contestants are expected to:

  • Be available to schedule fixtures within the 2-day scheduling window
  • Be available to complete scheduled fixtures within the 4-day match window
  • Be fully available on 30th and 31st August if the qualify for Finals

Failure to meet these availability requirements may result in match forfeiture.

Scoring System

Contestants accumulate Points during the Qualification stage of the tournament, allowing for the selection of the top 8 players for advancement to the Finals. These Points also help to determine each contestant's fixture match-ups when starting the Finals single elimination stage.

Points are awarded based on the following fixture outcome rules:

  • 1 Point - Fixture Win.
  • 0 Points - Fixture Loss.
  • 0 Points - Fixture No Show (after 15 minutes).

In the event of scoring ties, at the conclusion of the Qualification stage, tiebreakers will be decided based on a player's Opponent Match Win Percentage (OMW%).

Registration Process

Contestant slots are allocated on a first come first serve basis, with no registration fee, and once the registration window is open only a total of 32 players can register.

Once the registration window has opened, a 'Register Now' button will appear on the tournament's main page. Simply fill out this form with the appropriate information, and you will be contacted via Discord in order to confirm your placement.

Before registering to participate, please ensure you have read and understood the scheduling commitments and participation requirements. This will help to ensure that you have the appropriate availability to attend your scheduled fixtures and participate fully.
